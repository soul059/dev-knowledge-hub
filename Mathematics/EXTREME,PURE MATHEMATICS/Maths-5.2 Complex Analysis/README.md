# Complex Analysis — Comprehensive Study Notes

## Course Overview

**Complex Analysis** (also known as the theory of functions of a complex variable) is one of the most elegant and powerful branches of mathematics. It studies functions that map complex numbers to complex numbers, revealing deep connections between algebra, geometry, topology, and analysis.

This course covers the foundational theory from complex numbers through residue calculus and conformal mappings, building each concept logically upon the last. The subject is remarkable for its internal coherence: a single assumption — complex differentiability — leads to an extraordinary wealth of consequences including infinite differentiability, power series representations, and powerful integral formulas.

### Why Study Complex Analysis?

- **Physics & Engineering**: Fluid dynamics, electrostatics, heat conduction, quantum mechanics
- **Number Theory**: Prime number theorem, Riemann zeta function
- **Signal Processing**: Laplace and Fourier transforms, filter design
- **Applied Mathematics**: Evaluation of difficult real integrals, asymptotic analysis
- **Pure Mathematics**: Foundation for algebraic geometry, several complex variables

## Key Notation

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| $\mathbb{C}$ | Set of complex numbers |
| $\mathbb{C}^*$ | Extended complex plane $\mathbb{C} \cup \{\infty\}$ |
| $z = x + iy$ | A complex number with real part $x$, imaginary part $y$ |
| $\bar{z}$ | Complex conjugate of $z$ |
| $\|z\|$ | Modulus (absolute value) of $z$ |
| $\arg z$ | Argument (angle) of $z$ |
| $\text{Arg } z$ | Principal argument, $-\pi < \text{Arg } z \le \pi$ |
| $\text{Re}(z)$, $\text{Im}(z)$ | Real and imaginary parts |
| $f'(z_0)$ | Complex derivative of $f$ at $z_0$ |
| $\oint_C$ | Contour integral over closed curve $C$ |
| $\text{Res}_{z=z_0} f(z)$ | Residue of $f$ at $z_0$ |
| $D(z_0, r)$ | Open disk of radius $r$ centered at $z_0$ |
