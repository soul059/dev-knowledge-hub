## 1. Map Scales and Distances

### Understanding Scale
```
A map scale tells how distances on the map
relate to actual distances.

Scale 1 : 50,000 means:
1 cm on map = 50,000 cm in reality
1 cm on map = 500 m in reality
1 cm on map = 0.5 km in reality
```

### Types of Scale Representation
```
1. Ratio form:     1 : 25,000
2. Statement:      1 cm represents 250 m
3. Linear scale:   |â”€â”€|â”€â”€|â”€â”€|â”€â”€|
                   0  1  2  3  km
```

### Calculating Real Distance
```
Map scale: 1 : 200,000
Distance on map: 4.5 cm
Actual distance: ?

Actual = Map distance Ã— Scale factor
       = 4.5 Ã— 200,000 cm
       = 900,000 cm
       = 9 km
```

### Calculating Map Distance
```
Map scale: 1 : 50,000
Actual distance: 8 km
Map distance: ?

Map = Actual Ã· Scale factor
    = 8 km Ã· 50,000
    = 8,000 m Ã· 50,000
    = 800,000 cm Ã· 50,000
    = 16 cm
```

### Worked Example
```
On a map with scale 1 : 250,000:
a) Town A and B are 6 cm apart on map.
   What is the actual distance?

   Actual = 6 Ã— 250,000 = 1,500,000 cm
          = 15 km

   What is the map distance?
```

## 4. Partnership and Profit Sharing

### Equal Time Partnership
```
When partners invest for the SAME time period:

Profit shared in ratio of investments

A invests $3,000 and B invests $5,000 for 1 year.
Profit = $4,000

A's share : B's share = 3000 : 5000 = 3 : 5

Total parts = 8
A gets: 3/8 Ã— $4,000 = $1,500
B gets: 5/8 Ã— $4,000 = $2,500
```

### Different Time Partnership
```
When partners invest for DIFFERENT time periods:

Use: Investment Ã— Time

A invests $6,000 for 8 months
B invests $4,000 for 12 months

A's contribution = 6000 Ã— 8 = 48,000
B's contribution = 4000 Ã— 12 = 48,000

Ratio = 48,000 : 48,000 = 1 : 1

They share profit equally!
```

### Complex Partnership Example
```
A starts with $10,000
After 4 months, B joins with $15,000
Total profit after 12 months: $5,400

A's contribution = 10,000 Ã— 12 = 120,000
B's contribution = 15,000 Ã— 8 = 120,000

Ratio = 120,000 : 120,000 = 1 : 1

A gets: $5,400 Ã· 2 = $2,700
```

## 5. Time and Work Problems

### Basic Work Rate
```
If A can complete work in 10 days:
A's rate = 1/10 of work per day

If B can complete work in 15 days:
B's rate = 1/15 of work per day

Together per day = 1/10 + 1/15 = 3/30 + 2/30 = 5/30 = 1/6

Together they finish in 6 days
```

### Work Distribution
```
A and B can do a job in 12 days.
A alone takes 20 days.
How long for B alone?

Together: 1/12 per day
A alone: 1/20 per day

B's rate = 1/12 - 1/20
         = 5/60 - 3/60
         = 2/60 = 1/30

B alone takes 30 days
```

### Work-Wage Problems
```
If work is shared in ratio of work done:

A does 2/5 of work
B does 3/5 of work
Total wage: $250

A's wage = 2/5 Ã— $250 = $100
B's wage = 3/5 Ã— $250 = $150
```

## 7. Pipes and Cisterns

### Filling and Emptying
```
Pipe A fills a tank in 6 hours: Rate = 1/6 per hour
Pipe B fills a tank in 8 hours: Rate = 1/8 per hour
Pipe C empties in 12 hours: Rate = -1/12 per hour

All together:
Net rate = 1/6 + 1/8 - 1/12
         = 4/24 + 3/24 - 2/24
         = 5/24 per hour

Time to fill = 24/5 = 4.8 hours
```

### Partial Filling
```
A can fill in 10 hours, B can fill in 15 hours.
A works for 2 hours, then B joins.
How long to fill completely?

Work done by A in 2 hours = 2/10 = 1/5
Remaining work = 1 - 1/5 = 4/5

Together: 1/10 + 1/15 = 5/30 = 1/6 per hour

Time for 4/5 work = (4/5) Ã· (1/6) = 4/5 Ã— 6 = 24/5 hours

Total time = 2 + 24/5 = 34/5 = 6.8 hours
```
