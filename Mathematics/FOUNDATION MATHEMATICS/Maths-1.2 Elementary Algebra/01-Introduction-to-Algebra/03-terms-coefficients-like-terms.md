## 1. Terms in Algebraic Expressions

### Definition

A **term** is a single mathematical unit that can be:
- A constant (number)
- A variable
- A product of constants and variables

Terms are separated by addition (+) or subtraction (âˆ’) signs.

- The **same variable(s)**

### Derivation: Why It Works

The ability to combine like terms comes from the **Distributive Property**:

$$5x + 3x = x(5 + 3) = x \cdot 8 = 8x$$

Or more generally:

$$ax + bx = x(a + b) = (a + b)x$$

This is the reverse of distribution (also called **factoring out**).

### Examples of Combining Like Terms
