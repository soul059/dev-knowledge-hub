### Types of Motion Problems

**Type 1: Same Direction (Chase)**
```
Example: Car A leaves at 40 mph. One hour later, Car B leaves 
at 60 mph going the same direction. When does B catch A?

Let t = time Car B travels (hours)
Then t + 1 = time Car A travels

When B catches A, distances are equal:
60t = 40(t + 1)
60t = 40t + 40
20t = 40
t = 2 hours

Car B catches Car A after traveling for 2 hours.
```

**Type 2: Opposite Directions**
```
Example: Two trains leave a station at the same time, traveling 
in opposite directions. One goes 50 mph, the other 70 mph. 
When will they be 360 miles apart?

Combined rate = 50 + 70 = 120 mph
Distance apart = 360 miles

Time = Distance Ã· Rate = 360 Ã· 120 = 3 hours

Answer: After 3 hours
```

**Type 3: Round Trip**
```
Example: A cyclist rides uphill at 8 mph and returns downhill 
at 24 mph. If the round trip takes 4 hours, how far is the hilltop?

Let d = one-way distance

Time uphill = d/8
Time downhill = d/24
Total time = 4 hours

d/8 + d/24 = 4

Multiply by LCD (24):
3d + d = 96
```
