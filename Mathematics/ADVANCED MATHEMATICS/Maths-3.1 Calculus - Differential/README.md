## Course Overview

**Differential Calculus** is one of the two principal branches of calculus (the other being integral calculus). It deals with the study of **rates of change** and **slopes of curves**. Starting from the foundational concept of a *function*, we build through *limits*, *continuity*, and *differentiability* to arrive at the powerful machinery of *differentiation* â€” a tool that lets us analyze how quantities change, find optimal values, approximate functions, and sketch curves with precision.

This course takes a **theory-first, concept-building** approach. Every definition is motivated, every rule is derived, and every application is grounded in the mathematics that precedes it.

- Algebra (polynomials, factoring, exponents, logarithms)
- Trigonometry (identities, unit circle, inverse trig functions)
- Coordinate geometry (graphs, slopes, equations of lines)
