| Cardinality | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **1:1** | One to One | Person has Passport |
| **1:N** | One to Many | Dept has Employees |
| **M:N** | Many to Many | Students enroll Courses |

| Participation | Notation | Meaning |
|---------------|----------|---------|
| **Total** | Double line | Must participate |
| **Partial** | Single line | May not participate |

| Specialization | Disjoint (d) | Overlapping (o) |
|----------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Total** | Must be in exactly one subclass | Must be in at least one |
| **Partial** | May be in at most one | May be in any combination |

## â“ Quick Revision Questions

1. **What is the difference between an entity and an entity set?**
   <details>
   <summary>Click for Answer</summary>
   An entity is a specific, distinguishable thing in the real world (e.g., "Alice"). An entity set is a collection of similar entities (e.g., "STUDENT" containing all students).
   </details>

2. **What are the four types of attributes in ER model?**
   <details>
   <summary>Click for Answer</summary>
   (1) Simple vs Composite, (2) Single-valued vs Multi-valued, (3) Stored vs Derived, (4) Key attributes. Examples: Name is composite (First, Last), Phone is multi-valued, Age is derived from DOB, Stud_ID is key.
   </details>

3. **What makes an entity "weak"?**
   <details>
   <summary>Click for Answer</summary>
   A weak entity cannot be uniquely identified by its own attributes alone. It depends on an "owner" entity and has a partial key (discriminator). Example: DEPENDENT depends on EMPLOYEE.
   </details>

4. **Explain 1:N cardinality with an example.**
   <details>
   <summary>Click for Answer</summary>
   One-to-Many (1:N) means one entity on one side relates to many on the other. Example: One DEPARTMENT has many EMPLOYEEs, but each employee belongs to exactly one department.
   </details>

5. **What is the difference between total and partial participation?**
   <details>
   <summary>Click for Answer</summary>
   Total participation: Every entity MUST participate in the relationship (double line). Partial: Some entities MAY NOT participate (single line). Example: Every employee must work in a department (total), but an employee may not work on any project (partial).
   </details>

6. **Explain disjoint vs overlapping specialization.**
   <details>
   <summary>Click for Answer</summary>
   Disjoint (d): An entity can belong to only ONE subclass (Male OR Female). Overlapping (o): An entity can belong to MULTIPLE subclasses simultaneously (can be both Student AND Employee).
   </details>
