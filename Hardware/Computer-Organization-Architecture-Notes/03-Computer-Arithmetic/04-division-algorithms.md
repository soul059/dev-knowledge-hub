## â“ Quick Revision Questions

1. **Q**: What is the relationship between dividend, divisor, quotient, and remainder?
   
   **A**: Dividend = Quotient Ã— Divisor + Remainder, where 0 â‰¤ Remainder < |Divisor|.

2. **Q**: Why is the restoring division algorithm called "restoring"?
   
   **A**: Because when a trial subtraction produces a negative result, the original value is "restored" by adding the divisor back.

3. **Q**: What is the advantage of non-restoring over restoring division?
   
   **A**: Non-restoring requires only one add/subtract per iteration (+ final correction), while restoring may need two operations per iteration in worst case.

4. **Q**: How is division by zero typically handled in hardware?
   
   **A**: Hardware generates a division-by-zero exception/interrupt, which transfers control to an OS exception handler.

5. **Q**: In signed division, what are the signs of quotient and remainder for (-14) Ã· 3?
   
   **A**: Quotient = -4 (negative, since signs differ), Remainder = -2 (same sign as dividend). Verify: -14 = (-4) Ã— 3 + (-2) = -12 - 2 = -14 âœ“

6. **Q**: How many iterations does an n-bit division require?
   
   **A**: Both restoring and non-restoring require n iterations. Non-restoring may need one extra operation at the end for final correction.
