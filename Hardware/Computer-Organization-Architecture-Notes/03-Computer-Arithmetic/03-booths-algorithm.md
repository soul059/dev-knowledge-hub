## â“ Quick Revision Questions

1. **Q**: What is the key insight behind Booth's algorithm?
   
   **A**: A run of consecutive 1s can be replaced with a subtraction at the start and an addition at the end, reducing operations for numbers with long runs of 1s.

2. **Q**: For the bit pair (Qâ‚€, Qâ‚‹â‚) = (1, 0), what operation is performed?
   
   **A**: Subtract M (A = A - M). This indicates the beginning of a run of 1s.

3. **Q**: Why is arithmetic shift right used instead of logical shift right?
   
   **A**: Arithmetic shift preserves the sign bit, which is essential for correct signed multiplication. The MSB is replicated during the shift.

4. **Q**: How does radix-4 Booth improve upon standard Booth?
   
   **A**: Radix-4 examines 3 bits at a time and processes 2 bits per iteration, halving the number of cycles needed (n/2 instead of n).

5. **Q**: What are the possible partial products in radix-4 Booth?
   
   **A**: 0, +M, -M, +2M, -2M. The 2M values are obtained by simple left shift.
