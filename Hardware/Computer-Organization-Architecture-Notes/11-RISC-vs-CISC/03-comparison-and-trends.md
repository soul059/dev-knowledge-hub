   **A**: ARM's simple RISC design offers excellent power efficiency (critical for battery life), the licensing model enables innovation, and it scales from microcontrollers to high-performance cores.

4. **Q**: What makes RISC-V significant?
   
   **A**: RISC-V is an open-source ISA with no licensing fees. It has a modern, clean design, modular extensions, and is gaining adoption in education, embedded systems, and AI accelerators.

5. **Q**: What is the big.LITTLE concept?
   
   **A**: Combining high-performance cores (big) with power-efficient cores (LITTLE) in one SoC. Light tasks run on efficient cores; demanding tasks use performance cores. Optimizes power vs. performance.

6. **Q**: Has RISC definitively "won" over CISC?
   
   **A**: Not exactly. Modern x86 (CISC) processors are highly competitive by using RISC techniques internally. Both approaches have converged. x86 dominates desktops/servers; ARM dominates mobile. Market needs determine winner.
