# Chapter 3.5: Power Dissipation

## ğŸ“‹ Chapter Overview

**Power dissipation** is a critical concern in VLSI design. As technology scales and integration increases, managing power consumption becomes essential for thermal management, battery life, and reliability. This chapter analyzes the three main components of power in CMOS circuits: dynamic power, short-circuit power, and static (leakage) power.

---

## ğŸ¯ Learning Objectives

After completing this chapter, you will be able to:
- Identify and calculate the three components of CMOS power
- Understand the role of switching activity in dynamic power
- Explain short-circuit power and how to minimize it
- Analyze leakage mechanisms and their growing importance
- Apply power reduction techniques
