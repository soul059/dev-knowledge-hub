## â“ Quick Revision Questions

1. **Q:** What is the difference between BSS, ESS, and IBSS?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **BSS** (Basic Service Set):
   - Single AP + associated clients
   - Infrastructure mode
   - BSSID = AP's MAC address
   
   **ESS** (Extended Service Set):
   - Multiple APs with same SSID
   - Connected via distribution system
   - Enables roaming
   
   **IBSS** (Independent BSS):
   - Ad-hoc mode, no AP
   - Direct peer-to-peer
   - Limited use cases
   </details>

2. **Q:** Why does 802.11 use CSMA/CA instead of CSMA/CD?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **CSMA/CD requires collision detection** during transmission.
   
   **Wireless cannot do this because**:
   - Transmit power overwhelms receiver
   - Can't listen while transmitting
   - Half-duplex radio
   
   **CSMA/CA solution**:
   - Avoid collisions before they happen
   - Use random backoff
   - ACKs confirm success
   - RTS/CTS for hidden nodes
   </details>

3. **Q:** What is MIMO and MU-MIMO?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **MIMO** (Multiple Input Multiple Output):
   - Multiple antennas on AP and client
   - Sends parallel data streams
   - Uses multipath constructively
   - 4x4 MIMO = up to 4 streams
   
   **MU-MIMO** (Multi-User MIMO):
   - Serve multiple users simultaneously
   - Beamforming to each client
   - Requires Wi-Fi 5 (802.11ac) or later
   - Improves overall network capacity
   </details>

4. **Q:** Explain the CSMA/CA process step by step.
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **CSMA/CA Steps**:
   
   1. **Carrier Sense**: Check if channel is idle
   2. **If busy**: Wait until idle, then continue
   3. **Wait DIFS**: Mandatory waiting period
   4. **Random Backoff**: Random number of slots (collision avoidance)
   5. **Transmit**: Send the frame
   6. **Wait for ACK**: Receiver sends ACK after SIFS
   7. **If no ACK**: Increase backoff, retry
   
   Random backoff prevents simultaneous transmission when multiple stations wait.
   </details>

5. **Q:** What is the purpose of RTS/CTS?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **RTS/CTS solves hidden node problem**:
   
   **Problem**: Station A and B can't hear each other but both reach AP.
   
   **Solution**:
   1. Sender transmits RTS (Request to Send)
   2. AP responds with CTS (Clear to Send)
   3. CTS heard by ALL stations in range
   4. Hidden nodes know to wait (NAV timer)
   
   **Trade-off**: Adds overhead, typically used for large frames only.
   </details>

6. **Q:** Why does 802.11 have up to 4 MAC addresses?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **Four addresses handle different scenarios**:
   
   - **Address 1**: Receiver (who should process)
   - **Address 2**: Transmitter (who sent)
   - **Address 3**: Third party (source or dest)
   - **Address 4**: For WDS (wireless distribution)
   
   **To/From DS flags** indicate direction:
   - To DS=0, From DS=0: Ad-hoc
   - To DS=0, From DS=1: AP to station
   - To DS=1, From DS=0: Station to AP
   - To DS=1, From DS=1: WDS (AP to AP)
   
   Needed because wireless frame travels: Source â†’ AP â†’ Destination.
   </details>
