## ‚ùì Quick Revision Questions

1. **Q:** Why are channels 1, 6, and 11 commonly used in 2.4 GHz?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **Non-overlapping channels**:
   
   - 2.4 GHz band has only 11 channels in US (13 in EU)
   - Each channel is 22 MHz wide
   - Channels overlap with neighbors
   - Only 1, 6, 11 don't overlap each other
   
   Using overlapping channels causes interference between APs.
   
   Best practice: Use only 1, 6, 11 for multiple APs.
   </details>

2. **Q:** Compare 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands.
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **2.4 GHz**:
   - Longer range
   - Better wall penetration
   - Only 3 clean channels
   - More interference (microwaves, Bluetooth)
   - Lower maximum speeds
   
   **5 GHz**:
   - Shorter range
   - Poor wall penetration
   - 20+ clean channels
   - Less interference
   - Higher speeds possible
   
   Use 5 GHz when close to AP; 2.4 GHz for range.
   </details>

3. **Q:** What is RSSI and what values indicate good signal?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **RSSI** = Received Signal Strength Indicator
   
   Measured in dBm (more negative = weaker):
   - -30 dBm: Excellent (very close to AP)
   - -50 dBm: Very good
   - -60 dBm: Good (reliable connection)
   - -70 dBm: Fair (some issues possible)
   - -80 dBm: Poor (frequent drops)
   - -90 dBm: Very poor (barely usable)
   
   Target: -65 dBm or better for reliable operation.
   </details>

4. **Q:** What is the hidden node problem?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **Hidden Node Problem**:
   
   Two clients (A and B) can both reach AP but cannot hear each other.
   
   Issue:
   - A doesn't know B is transmitting
   - B doesn't know A is transmitting
   - Both transmit simultaneously
   - Collision occurs at AP
   
   **Solution**: RTS/CTS (Request to Send / Clear to Send)
   - Before transmitting, send RTS
   - AP responds with CTS heard by all
   - Other nodes know to wait
   </details>

5. **Q:** Why is wireless half-duplex?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **Wireless is half-duplex** because:
   
   1. **Shared medium**: All devices use same frequency
   2. **Can't detect while transmitting**: Radio output overwhelms receiver
   3. **One transmitter at a time**: To avoid collisions
   
   Contrast with wired:
   - Ethernet switches provide full-duplex
   - Separate TX and RX wire pairs
   
   This is why wireless is inherently slower than theoretical max - only one device talks at a time.
   </details>

6. **Q:** What causes multipath and what are its effects?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **Multipath**: Signal reaches receiver via multiple paths
   
   **Causes**:
   - Reflection off walls, ceiling, floor
   - Reflection off metal objects
   - Diffraction around obstacles
   
   **Effects**:
   - **Negative**: Signals arrive out of phase ‚Üí interference, dead spots
   - **Positive**: MIMO technology uses multipath to increase speed
   
   MIMO (Multiple Input Multiple Output) uses multiple antennas to exploit multipath for parallel data streams.
   </details>
