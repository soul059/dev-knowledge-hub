## â“ Quick Revision Questions

1. **Q:** What is the difference between Bluetooth Classic and BLE?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **Bluetooth Classic**:
   - Continuous connection
   - Higher data rates (3 Mbps)
   - Audio streaming, file transfer
   - Higher power consumption
   
   **Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE)**:
   - Short bursts of data
   - Lower data rates (2 Mbps)
   - Sensors, beacons, wearables
   - Very low power (years on coin cell)
   
   BLE designed specifically for IoT applications.
   </details>

2. **Q:** Explain MQTT publish/subscribe model.
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **MQTT Architecture**:
   
   - **Publisher**: Sends messages to topics
   - **Broker**: Central server, routes messages
   - **Subscriber**: Receives messages from topics
   
   **How it works**:
   1. Subscriber subscribes to topic "temp/room1"
   2. Publisher publishes to "temp/room1" with value
   3. Broker forwards to all subscribers
   
   **Benefits**:
   - Decouples sender and receiver
   - One-to-many distribution
   - Lightweight protocol
   </details>

3. **Q:** What makes Zigbee suitable for smart home?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **Zigbee advantages for smart home**:
   
   - **Mesh networking**: Devices relay messages, extends range
   - **Low power**: Years on battery
   - **Many devices**: Up to 65,000 nodes
   - **Security**: 128-bit AES encryption
   - **Interoperability**: Standard protocol
   
   **Device types**:
   - Coordinator: Forms network
   - Router: Extends range (always on)
   - End device: Sleeps, battery-powered
   </details>

4. **Q:** How does LoRa achieve such long range?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **LoRa long range techniques**:
   
   - **Sub-GHz frequency** (868/915 MHz): Better penetration than 2.4 GHz
   - **Chirp Spread Spectrum**: Spreading signal for noise immunity
   - **Very low data rate**: Trade speed for range
   - **Narrow bandwidth**: Concentrates power
   
   **Trade-off**:
   - 15+ km range possible
   - But only 0.3-50 kbps data rate
   - Suitable for small sensor readings, not video
   </details>

5. **Q:** Compare MQTT and CoAP for IoT.
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **MQTT**:
   - TCP-based (reliable)
   - Publish/Subscribe model
   - Requires broker
   - Good for: Telemetry, status updates
   
   **CoAP**:
   - UDP-based (lightweight)
   - Request/Response (like HTTP)
   - No broker needed
   - Good for: Resource access, constrained devices
   
   **Choose MQTT** for many sensors to central system.
   **Choose CoAP** for direct device communication.
   </details>

6. **Q:** What are the LoRaWAN device classes?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **Class A** (All devices support):
   - Transmit anytime
   - Receive only after transmit (2 short windows)
   - Lowest power consumption
   - Highest latency
   
   **Class B**:
   - Scheduled receive windows (beacon-synchronized)
   - Medium power and latency
   
   **Class C**:
   - Continuous receive (always listening)
   - Highest power
   - Lowest latency
   - Usually mains-powered
   
   Most sensors use Class A for battery life.
   </details>
