## â“ Quick Revision Questions

1. **Q:** What is the main reason for twisting the wires in twisted pair cables?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   The twisting helps **cancel out electromagnetic interference (EMI)** and reduces **crosstalk** between adjacent wire pairs. Each twist creates opposite magnetic fields that cancel out, protecting the signal from external interference and preventing the wires from interfering with each other.
   </details>

2. **Q:** What are the key differences between UTP and STP cables?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **UTP (Unshielded Twisted Pair)**: No metallic shielding, less expensive, easier to install, more flexible, but more susceptible to EMI. Used in offices and homes.
   **STP (Shielded Twisted Pair)**: Has metal foil or braid shielding, more expensive, harder to install, less flexible, but provides better EMI protection. Used in industrial environments with high interference.
   </details>

3. **Q:** What is the difference between single-mode and multi-mode fiber optic cable?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **Single-mode fiber**: Very thin core (~9 Î¼m), uses LASER light, one light path, supports distances up to 100 km, higher cost, used for long-distance WANs.
   **Multi-mode fiber**: Wider core (50/62.5 Î¼m), uses LED light, multiple light paths, supports distances up to 2 km, lower cost, used for LANs and data centers.
   </details>

4. **Q:** Why do microwaves require line-of-sight transmission?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   Microwaves are **unidirectional** (focused beam) and have **short wavelengths** that cannot bend around or penetrate solid obstacles like buildings or mountains. They travel in straight lines, so the transmitter and receiver must have a clear, unobstructed path between them. This is why microwave towers are placed on high ground.
   </details>

5. **Q:** What are the advantages of fiber optic cable over copper cables?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   Fiber optic advantages:
   - **Much higher bandwidth** (100+ Gbps)
   - **Immunity to electromagnetic interference**
   - **Longer transmission distances** (up to 100 km)
   - **Better security** (difficult to tap)
   - **Lighter weight** and thinner cables
   - **No electrical grounding** issues
   - **No signal radiation** (doesn't cause interference)
   </details>

6. **Q:** Which transmission medium would you recommend for connecting two buildings 500m apart in an area with heavy electrical interference?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **Fiber optic cable** would be the best choice because:
   - 500m is within the range of multi-mode fiber (up to 2 km)
   - Fiber is completely immune to electromagnetic interference
   - Fiber provides high bandwidth for future needs
   - Though more expensive, it offers the most reliable solution
   
   Alternative: Microwave link if running cable is impractical.
   </details>
