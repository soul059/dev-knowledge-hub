## â“ Quick Revision Questions

1. **Q:** What are the three elements that a protocol defines?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   A protocol defines:
   1. **Syntax** - The format and structure of data
   2. **Semantics** - The meaning of each field and actions to take
   3. **Timing** - When to send, how fast, and in what order
   </details>

2. **Q:** What is the difference between de jure and de facto standards?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   **De jure** standards are created by official standards organizations (like IEEE 802.3 for Ethernet). **De facto** standards become standard through widespread adoption and use (like TCP/IP, which became the Internet standard through practical use before formal standardization).
   </details>

3. **Q:** What does IEEE 802.11 standardize?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   IEEE 802.11 standardizes Wireless LAN (WiFi) technology, including various amendments like 802.11a/b/g/n/ac/ax (WiFi 4/5/6) that define different speeds, frequencies, and capabilities.
   </details>

4. **Q:** What are RFCs and which organization publishes them?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   RFCs (Request for Comments) are documents published by the IETF (Internet Engineering Task Force) that define Internet protocols and standards. Despite the name, they are official technical specifications for protocols like TCP, IP, HTTP, and DNS.
   </details>

5. **Q:** Name three major standards organizations and their focus areas.
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   1. **IEEE** - LAN/MAN standards (Ethernet 802.3, WiFi 802.11)
   2. **IETF** - Internet protocols (TCP, IP, HTTP, DNS)
   3. **ITU-T** - Telecommunications standards (modems, DSL)
   (Also valid: ISO - OSI model, W3C - Web standards)
   </details>

6. **Q:** What is the naming convention for Ethernet standards like "1000BASE-T"?
   <details>
   <summary>Answer</summary>
   The format is [Speed][Signaling]-[Media]:
   - **1000** = Speed in Mbps (1000 Mbps = 1 Gbps)
   - **BASE** = Baseband signaling
   - **T** = Media type (T = Twisted pair, F = Fiber)
   
   So 1000BASE-T = 1 Gbps over twisted pair using baseband signaling.
   </details>
